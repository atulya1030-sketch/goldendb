GoldenDB guide

What each link is and why it exists

GoldenDB has five pages. Two are client-facing — anyone can use them. Three are internal — for the GoldenDB team only to build and manage the candidate pool.

/ Client-facing
Recruiter Chat

The main product. A recruiter or hiring manager types what they're looking for — "gold band ML engineer in Bengaluru, 30-day notice, under 50L" — and gets back a shortlist of verified candidates with links to their full profiles. It can also answer market questions like "which city has the most relocatable fintech engineers?" or build a full team from scratch.

Why this exists
Recruiters don't think in filters and dropdowns. They think in natural language. This lets them ask exactly what they mean and get a precise, reasoned answer instantly — instead of manually scanning spreadsheets or running Boolean searches on LinkedIn.
/profile/GDB-1000 Client-facing
Candidate Profile

Every candidate in GoldenDB has a profile page at /profile/GDB-XXXX. When the recruiter chat mentions a candidate, it links here. The page has two tabs — Client Profile (what the client sees: skills, experience, availability, compensation, systems built) and Internal Signals (recruiter-only: scoring breakdown, risk flags, panel notes, retention prediction).

Why this exists
A chat response can only say so much. When a recruiter wants to go deep on someone before presenting them to a client, this page has everything — verified by panel, organised clearly, with infographics for depth and skills. It's the document a recruiter walks into a client meeting with.
/source-chat Internal only
Sourcing Copilot

A chat interface for the GoldenDB team to find new candidates. You describe who you're looking for, and it searches GitHub, scores the profiles, fetches contact info (email, phone, LinkedIn) via Apollo, and presents results. You can then say "save the top 3" and they get added to the pipeline — ready to be contacted, have their resume uploaded, and eventually become client-ready.

Why this exists
GitHub is the most honest signal for an AI/data engineer — it shows what they've actually built, not just what they've claimed on a CV. This tool automates the discovery and contact-finding step so the team spends time on outreach and interviews, not manual searching.
/source Internal only
Form-Based Sourcing

The same sourcing pipeline as /source-chat but as a search form instead of a conversation. Type a query, hit Search, and candidate cards stream in one by one — each with their GitHub stats, contact info, and an "Add to GoldenDB" button. Useful when you want to scan results visually rather than have a back-and-forth conversation.

Why this exists
Some people prefer seeing all results laid out as cards rather than in a chat. Both /source and /source-chat do the same thing — use whichever feels more natural to you.
/upload Internal only
Resume Upload

Upload a candidate's resume PDF and GoldenDB extracts all the structured fields automatically — skills, experience, systems built, education, compensation expectations, notice period, and more. You can also provide their GitHub, Kaggle, Stack Overflow, or HuggingFace handles and it enriches the profile from those platforms in the same step. Once done, the candidate is in the database and searchable.

Why this exists
When a candidate sends us their CV, we don't want to manually fill out a 50-field schema. This page does it in under 10 seconds using GPT-4o. It's how a stage-1 sourced candidate (just a GitHub profile) becomes a stage-2 candidate with a full structured record.
